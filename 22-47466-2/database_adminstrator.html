Database Administrator

Summary:

Age: 28 to 45 years

Experience: At least 4 years

Salary: Negotiable

Minimum qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Information Systems, or a related field.
  • Strong knowledge of database systems such as MySQL, PostgreSQL, Oracle, or SQL Server.
  • Experience in database installation, configuration, and maintenance.
  • Proficiency in SQL and database scripting.
  • Experience in database backup, recovery, and disaster recovery strategies.

Preferred qualifications:

  • Master's degree in Information Technology, Computer Science, or related field.
  • Experience with database performance tuning and optimization.
  • Knowledge of cloud database solutions (AWS RDS, Google Cloud SQL, Azure SQL Database).
  • Experience in database security and compliance standards.
  • Familiarity with NoSQL databases such as MongoDB or Cassandra.

About the job:

As a Database Administrator (DBA), you will be responsible for managing and maintaining the organization’s database systems to ensure high availability, performance, and security. You will collaborate with developers, system administrators, and analysts to design and implement database solutions that support critical business applications.

Your role will include monitoring database performance, troubleshooting issues, and implementing backup and disaster recovery strategies. You will also enforce data security policies and ensure compliance with industry standards.

The US base salary range for this full-time position is $100,000–$150,000 + bonus + benefits, depending on experience and qualifications.

Responsibilities:

  • Install, configure, and upgrade database servers and applications.
  • Monitor and optimize database performance, ensuring efficiency and reliability.
  • Implement security measures to safeguard sensitive data.
  • Plan and implement backup, recovery, and disaster recovery procedures.
  • Troubleshoot database issues and provide timely resolutions.
  • Collaborate with development teams to optimize queries and database structures.
  • Maintain documentation of database environments and procedures.
